1. Q: What is the main function of the rough ER?
   A. Lipid synthesis
   B. Protein synthesis and folding
   C. Energy production
   D. Calcium storage
   Correct Answer: Protein synthesis and folding

2. Q: Which feature distinguishes rough ER from smooth ER?
   A. Its coiled shape
   B. Presence of ribosomes on its surface
   C. Its location in the cytoplasm
   D. It stores DNA
   Correct Answer: Presence of ribosomes on its surface

3. Q: The smooth ER is primarily involved in:
   A. Protein folding
   B. Lipid synthesis and detoxification
   C. DNA replication
   D. Cell division
   Correct Answer: Lipid synthesis and detoxification

4. Q: Which type of ER helps detoxify drugs and poisons in the liver?
   A. Rough ER
   B. Smooth ER
   C. Golgi apparatus
   D. Nucleolus
   Correct Answer: Smooth ER

5. Q: Where are proteins synthesized for export from the cell?
   A. Smooth ER
   B. Golgi apparatus
   C. Rough ER
   D. Cytosol
   Correct Answer: Rough ER

6. Q: Which organelle is continuous with the nuclear envelope?
   A. Lysosome
   B. Mitochondria
   C. Endoplasmic reticulum
   D. Ribosome
   Correct Answer: Endoplasmic reticulum

7. Q: Which ER stores calcium in muscle cells?
   A. Rough ER
   B. Smooth ER
   C. Golgi
   D. Mitochondria
   Correct Answer: Smooth ER

8. Q: Which type of ER packages newly made proteins for transport?
   A. Smooth ER
   B. Rough ER
   C. Golgi apparatus
   D. Nucleolus
   Correct Answer: Rough ER

9. Q: Which ER lacks ribosomes and is smooth in appearance?
   A. Rough ER
   B. Nucleolus
   C. Smooth ER
   D. Golgi apparatus
   Correct Answer: Smooth ER

10. Q: Which organelle is more involved in synthesizing membrane-bound proteins?
   A. Smooth ER
   B. Nucleus
   C. Rough ER
   D. Ribosomes
   Correct Answer: Rough ER
